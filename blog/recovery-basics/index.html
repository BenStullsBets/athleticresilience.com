Recovery basics: three habits that keep you in the game

You don't need fancy gadgets to recover well. You need a few simple habits, done consistently. Here are three we come back to again and again.

1. Release tension before it becomes pain

Tight tissue doesn't just feel bad — it changes how you move, and that's where small issues turn into injuries. A few minutes of targeted self-treatment a day keeps your tissue supple and your movement clean. The body works in fascial patterns that connect you head to toe, so releasing one area often frees up another.

2. Train movement, not just muscles

Strength that holds up in real life comes from training the way your body actually moves — full ranges, real positions, and progressions you can sustain. The goal isn't to crush yourself once; it's to show up tomorrow and the day after.

3. Recover on purpose

Recovery isn't the absence of training — it's part of it. Sleep, simple mobility work, and knowing when to back off are what let you perform when it counts. Build the habit, and resilience follows.
